Falafel

Is a deep-fried ball of Egyptian origin, featuring in Middle Eastern cuisine, particularly Levantine cuisines.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1/2 tbs baking soda
  • 1 cup fresh parsley leaves
  • 1 small onion
  • 7-8 garlic cloves
  • 2 tbs tahini
  • 1 tbs ground cumin
  • salt to taste

Recipe instructions:

  1. Soak your dried chickpeas overnight or for at least 8 to 12 hours.
  2. Add all the ingredients to a food processor. I recommend roughly chopping up the onion, herbs and pepper before adding. Pulse the food processor but do not blend completely. The final mixture should resemble coarse sand.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a bowl then add the chickpea flour and baking soda, stir it together until it is fully combined and cover with plastic wrap or a lid.
  4. Place the bowl in the fridge. Chill the falafel mixture for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  5. Shape your falafel. You can do this by hand or with a cookie scoop and then deep fry it at 175Â°C.
